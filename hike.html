Hikes

Reintal Valley Trail from Partnach Gorge to Sonnalpin Station & Zugspitze

The hike from Garmisch-Partenkirchen Olympic Stadium up the back of the 2,962m (9,718ft) summit of Zugspitze is both awe-inspiring and challenging. The route is considered safe and not technically demanding - suitable for any fit hiker. But it is rated hard in difficulty due to its length 21.7km (13.5mi) and elevation gain 2,200m (7,218ft). The first section of trail passes through the picturesque Partnach Gorge followed by a long gentle ascent through the scenic Reintal Valley. After passing the Reintalangerhütte (9mi) the trail sharply ascends past treeline into rocky alpine terrain. About 1km past the Knorrhütte (11mi), the trail begins to flatten out and alternate between a more gentle uphill and flatter sections. At the Sonnalpin Station (12.5mi), hikers may opt to ride the tram the rest of the way up, or if enough time and leg stamina remain, continue by foot. The final section continues up the large scree field and crosses onto a more solid but exposed rocky section with anchored cables as safety handles, requiring increased caution if snow is present. This scree field and rocky section is the hardest part, but hikers who persist are rewarded with spectacular views.
