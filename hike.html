The Hike

Reintal Valley Route

The hike from Garmisch-Partenkirchen Olympic Stadium up Reintal Valley to the top of Zugspitze is both awe-inspiring and challenging. The Reintal Valley route is considered safe and not technically demanding - suitable for any fit hiker. But it is rated hard in difficulty due to its length 21.7km (13.5mi) and elevation gain 2,200m (7,218ft). The first section of trail passes through the picturesque Partnach Gorge followed by a long gentle ascent through the scenic Reintal Valley. After passing the Reintalangerhütte (9mi) the trail sharply ascends past treeline into rocky alpine terrain. About 1km past the Knorrhütte (11mi), the trail begins to flatten out and alternate between a more gentle uphill and flatter sections. At the Sonnalpin Station (12.5mi), hikers may opt to ride the tram the rest of the way up, or if enough time and leg stamina remain, continue by foot. The final section continues up the large scree field and crosses onto a more solid but exposed rocky section with anchored cables as safety handles, requiring increased caution if snow is present. This scree field and rocky section is the hardest part, but hikers who persist are rewarded with spectacular views.

Gatterl Route

The Gatterl route is a shorter alternative to the Reintal Valley route, but may require additional planning and coordination due to several factors. First, the Gatterl route passes through some steeper and exposed terrain that may be more dangerous for less experienced mountaineers, especially if there is snow or ice present. Second, the Gatterl route begins in Austria, requiring vehicular transportation and consideration of parking fees and car retrieval, or increased planning for public transit. Third, hikers can save time ascending the Gatterl route by beginning at the top of the Ahrwalder Almbahn cable car, requiring additional tickets and timing considerations. is less popular and less well-known than the Reintal Valley route, so there may be less information available about it, such as trail conditions, weather forecasts, and potential hazards. Finally, the Gatterl route may require additional permits or permissions to access certain areas, which could add complexity to the planning process. Overall, while the Gatterl route offers a more direct and potentially faster ascent to the summit of Zugspitze, it may not be suitable for all hikers due to its increased difficulty and potential logistical challenges.
